Core Expertise

We bridge the gap between theoretical computer science and real-world application through three specialized wings.

Web Development

We architect scalable digital ecosystems. Our team specializes in the MERN stack, Next.js, and Docker. We maintain the college's official result portals and have built event platforms handling over 10,000 concurrent requests.

AI & Machine Learning

Moving beyond basic models, we focus on Deep Learning and Generative AI. Our research groups are currently working on LLM fine-tuning, Computer Vision for autonomous drones, and predictive analytics for local businesses.

Quant Finance

The intersection of math and money. We simulate High-Frequency Trading (HFT) strategies using C++ and Python. Members learn Stochastic Calculus, Algorithmic Trading, and backtesting strategies.

Flagship Project: SQC-Dog

SQC-Dog is not just a robot; it is a research platform. Built entirely from scratch by our hardware team, it serves as a testbed for Reinforcement Learning (RL) and Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM).

SQC-Dog Technical Architecture

Version: Mark IV | OS: ROS 2 Humble

Hardware

Raspberry Pi 4 (8GB) for high-level logic. STM32 for motor control via UART.

Perception

OAK-D Depth Camera for SLAM and Object Detection. Lidar for mapping.

Kinematics

Inverse Kinematics (IK) algorithms calculate joint angles for 12 Degrees of Freedom.

Gait Control

Bezier curve trajectory planning for smooth trot and crawl gaits.

Working Principle:

The robot uses a State Machine architecture. The Raspberry Pi processes vision data to determine the path. It sends velocity commands to the Gait Controller. The Controller solves the Inverse Kinematics equations to position the feet (X,Y,Z) and sends angle targets to the 12 servo motors 50 times per second.
